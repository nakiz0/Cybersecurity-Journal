# CIA Triad

## What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is the foundation of information security and cybersecurity.

It consists of three core security principles:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

Every security control, policy, tool, and technology is designed to protect one or more of these principles.

The CIA Triad serves as the framework for securing systems, networks, applications, and data.

---

# Why the CIA Triad Matters

Organizations store and process:

- Personal Information
- Financial Records
- Medical Data
- Intellectual Property
- Business Information

The CIA Triad helps ensure this information remains:

- Private
- Accurate
- Accessible

A failure in any of these areas can result in security incidents, data breaches, or service disruptions.

---

# Confidentiality

## Definition

Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized individuals.

Unauthorized users should not be able to view sensitive information.

---

## Goal

Prevent unauthorized disclosure of data.

---

## Examples

### Banking

Only the account owner should see account details.

### Healthcare

Patient records should only be accessible to authorized medical staff.

### Corporate Environment

Employees should only access information relevant to their roles.

---

## Security Controls

### Encryption

Protects data from unauthorized access.

Examples:

- AES
- RSA
- TLS

---

### Access Control

Restricts access based on permissions.

Examples:

- RBAC
- ACLs
- IAM Policies

---

### Authentication

Verifies user identity.

Examples:

- Passwords
- MFA
- Biometrics

---

## Threats to Confidentiality

- Data Breaches
- Insider Threats
- Eavesdropping
- Phishing
- Credential Theft

---

# Integrity

## Definition

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unmodified.

Information should not be altered without authorization.

---

## Goal

Prevent unauthorized modification of data.

---

## Examples

### Banking

Account balances must remain accurate.

### Software Development

Source code should not be modified by unauthorized individuals.

### Medical Systems

Patient records must remain unchanged unless updated by authorized personnel.

---

## Security Controls

### Hashing

Verifies data integrity.

Examples:

- SHA-256
- SHA-512

---

### Digital Signatures

Verify authenticity and integrity.

Examples:

- RSA Signatures
- ECC Signatures

---

### Version Control

Tracks changes to files and code.

Examples:

- Git
- GitHub

---

## Threats to Integrity

- Malware
- SQL Injection
- Unauthorized Changes
- Data Corruption
- Man-in-the-Middle Attacks

---

# Availability

## Definition

Availability ensures that systems, services, and data remain accessible when needed.

Authorized users should be able to access resources without interruption.

---

## Goal

Prevent service disruption and downtime.

---

## Examples

### Websites

Users should be able to access services 24/7.

### Cloud Services

Applications should remain operational even during failures.

### Banking Systems

ATMs and online banking should remain available.

---

## Security Controls

### Backups

Ensure recovery after failures.

### Redundancy

Multiple systems provide fault tolerance.

### Load Balancing

Distributes traffic across servers.

### Disaster Recovery Plans

Provide recovery procedures after incidents.

---

## Threats to Availability

- DDoS Attacks
- Hardware Failures
- Power Outages
- Ransomware
- Natural Disasters

---

# Key Takeaway

The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity.

Every security control ultimately aims to protect:

## Confidentiality
Protect information from unauthorized access.

## Integrity
Ensure information remains accurate and trustworthy.

## Availability
Ensure systems and data remain accessible when needed.

Understanding the CIA Triad provides the foundation for cloud security, network security, cryptography, secure software development, risk management, and cybersecurity as a whole.
